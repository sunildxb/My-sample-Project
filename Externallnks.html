Ekata

EKATA (Expatriate Keralites’ Association for Tradition & Arts) is an association of like-minded Indian citizens from the south Indian state Kerala (Malayalees) living in UAE. It stands for upholding India’s great tradition, culture, heritage and provides an avenue for the next generation to understand their native language Malayalam, Kerala, country, history, culture and tradition with the foremost vision being uplift of the downtrodden among the society through social service and charity. Prior to formalizing EKATA as an association, a group of people had special interest in India’s ancient culture, tradition, spirituality and gathered on weekends to share their learnings to benefit among them. This led some of the members voluntarily to educate and enlighten the children about India’s rich heritage and culture, such as:- An insight into the epics, sacred scriptures, Bhagavat Gita and so on Introducing social values dear to Indians and their tradition Yoga, classical art forms, music Mother tongue Malayalam Such an informal gathering of like-minded people was transformed into an association and was named "EKATA" (Expatriate Keralites’ Association for Tradition & Arts) during the inaugural ceremony held in Sharjah on April 20, 2007. The group has always been in the forefront to contribute towards charity and social cause. Members are encouraged to make voluntary donations towards the needy and the noble causes from time to time.
